**Why my dissertation matters here.** The Economic Index measures what tasks people use AI for. My dissertation identifies something underneath that: the same model produces different reasoning depending on how the prompt is framed. "Consider your values" produces 0% defection in my prisoner's dilemma experiments. "Make your choice now" produces 83%. That's not noise. My scoring system can detect which reasoning mode a model is in from response text using pattern matching alone. Applied to Clio's privacy-preserving usage data, that's a way to study not just what people ask AI to do, but how the model is actually reasoning when it responds.

**Policy evaluation, not advocacy.** My strength is causal measurement of policy impacts, not lobbying. The CJL work on zero bail and policing bias shows the pattern: use quasi-experimental methods to measure what actually happened, then let the estimates inform the conversation.
